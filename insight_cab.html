Insight for Cab Investment Firm

A comprehensive analysis that delves deep into the cab industry, aiding XYZ, a private firm in the US, in making informed investment decisions. The project merges two key sections that involve Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and a solution-driven overview, offering insights that lead to actionable recommendations.

Project Overview and Solution:
XYZ, a private firm in the US, is contemplating a strategic investment in the booming Cab Industry. The proliferation of the industry and the presence of multiple key players have prompted the firm to adopt a cautious Go-to-Market (G2M) strategy. In response, this project seeks to provide a comprehensive analysis of the cab market by examining historical data from major cab services across significant US cities. The primary objective is to furnish XYZ with actionable insights that facilitate well-informed investment decisions.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and Insights:
1. Dataset Description and Basic Statistics:
The dataset underpinning this analysis comprises approximately 360,000 records, encompassing 22 attributes from two distinct cab services across 19 US cities. The project commenced with preliminary data scrutiny, including the identification of null and duplicate values.
2. Comparative Analysis Across Cities:
The core of the EDA involved comparing the performance of the two cab services across diverse cities. The analysis encompassed various dimensions, including:
- Profits generated by each cab service
- Distance traveled on weekdays and weekends
- Monthly variations in performance
- Distribution of male and female customers for each cab service
3. Hypothesis Testing for Informed Recommendations:
To buttress the insights gained from the EDA, rigorous hypothesis testing was employed. The statistical analysis fortified the findings and enabled well-founded recommendations to emerge.

Key Findings and Recommendations:
Based on the comprehensive analysis, several noteworthy findings surfaced:
- Yellow Cab demonstrated dominance across most cities in terms of user preference and count.
- The profit margin of Yellow Cab consistently outperformed Pink Cab over the year.
- Sacramento emerged as a city where the profit per kilometer was significantly lower, and Yellow Cab was not the preferred choice.
- The majority of cab users fell within the 20 to 40 age bracket.
- Holidays appeared to exert negligible impact on profit and distance traveled for both cab service providers.

Future Enhancement:
While the analysis provides valuable insights, opportunities for future enhancement remain. Integrating predictive modeling to forecast trends and exploring socio-economic factors that influence user preferences could further enrich the analysis.
